WORLD OF DANCE INTERNATIONAL 2026 CHAMPIONSHIP SERIES IN LATVIA

23 MAY 2026

Judging

Competitions will be judged according to the World of Dance standards with a 5-panel jury for Team Division, Junior Team Division and Studio Division.

Judging will maintain an Olympic style points structure, where top and bottom scores are removed. The 3 remaining judge scores are averaged and produce one final team score.

WOD CHAMPIONSHIP SERIES

Advancing Divisions

There are 2 divisions that qualify for World of Dance Finals:

  1. Junior Team Division
  2. Team Division

The following details outline how dancers are qualified for each division:

Junior Team Division

  • Ages: under 18
  • Group size: 5+
  • Experience & Background:

    Dancers have performance experience in a pre-professional, community, or industry competition setting. Dancers exhibit consistent and efficient understanding of formations and use of stage. Complexity of choreography is evident and demonstrated throughout the set.

Team Division

  • Ages: If the group consists of any dancers over the age of 18* the group will compete in the Team division
  • Group size: 5+
  • Experience & Background:

    Dancers have performance experience in professional, pre-professional, community or industry competition setting. Dancers exhibit an efficient understanding of intricate formations and use of stage. Complexity of choreography, originality and exceptional creativity is evident and demonstrated throughout the set.

Dancer Registration

All dancers that register as a team, must include their team name, primary contact, email, and phone number, so that all participants can be invited to the World Dance Summit, a multi-day event featuring the World of Dance Finals Competition, The World Showcase, The Summit Games, and The Master Classes.

Winners

Competitors that finish in first, second, or third place in the Junior Team Division and Team Division at the Regional (hereinafter referred to as “Regional Winners”) are qualified to participate as finalists in the World of Dance Finals Competition event. If first, second, or third place teams are unable to attend, the runner up teams will be invited as Regional Winners in their place. The World Finals Competition takes place during the summer months of July and/or August in California, USA.

Representation

Qualifying Junior Team Division and Team Division winners are invited to participate in the World of Dance Finals competition. Teams must represent the country of the Regional Event in which they participated in. If teams have qualified in more than one country, the team may select which country they chose to represent. All travel, accommodations, and registration fees shall be the responsibility of the Regional Winners. Regional Winners will be provided with official memorandums or VISA invites, if necessary to procure international travel approval.

WOD Styles judging system

Criteria

All judges will evaluate each team according to the following criteria:

  1. Execution: 20% (max. 20 points out of 100)
    • energy
    • stage performance
    • ability to impress
  2. Technique: 20% (max. 20 points out of 100)
    • clarity of dance
    • dynamics of movements
    • execution
    • basics of style
    • body control
    • musicality / timing synchronization
  3. Choreography: 20% (20 points out of 100)
    • originality of the movement
    • creativity of choreography
    • concept
    • variety of techniques
    • musicality
  4. Creativity: 20% (max. 20 points out of 100)
    • music selection
    • movement
    • theme
    • performance
  5. Presentation: 20% ( max. 20 points out of 100)
    • costume
    • hairstyle and makeup
    • attitude/expression

Calculation methodology

Depending on the number of judges on the panel, 2 different counting principles are used:

  • If there are 1-4 judges: Simple system
  • If there are 5 or more judges: Olympic system

Simple system

  1. The scores for the criteria are summarized for each judge separately. This is how the judge's score is obtained.
  2. The scores of all judges are averaged and a single final score is displayed.
  3. If there are penalty points, the sum of the penalty points is subtracted from the final score.

Olympic system

  1. The scores for the criteria are summed up for each judge separately. This is how we get the judge's score.
  2. The maximum and minimum scores are subtracted from all judges' scores.
  3. All remaining judges' scores are averaged to get one final score.

Example:

  • judge 1 total score: 60
  • judge 2 total score: 70
  • judge 3 total score: 80
  • judge 4 total score: 90
  • judge 5 total score: 40

Crossed out: 90 (judge 4) and 40 (judge 5)

Total score of the dance team: (60+70+80) / 3 judges = 70 points

Deduction points

There are 4 specific categories that we provide point deductions on

  • Profanity in music - 0.5 point deduction per curse word
  • Unapproved Props - 1 point deduction per judge (up to a total of 5 points deduction to final score)
  • Music longer than 6 minutes - 0.5 point deduction in total
  • Poorly Executed Tricks - 1 point deduction per trick

Deduction points will be subtracted from the participant's overall average score (final score)

Score divisions

According to the World of Dance rules, there are only one 1st, 2nd and 3rd place in each category of competition.

One prize place = One winner/team of winners.

If two (or more) teams/participants score the same number of points for one of the prize places, a tie is declared.

In case of a tie, the tiebreaker will be as follows:

  • Average of technique scores. If tie still remains,
  • Average of performance scores. If tie still remains,
  • Average of choreography scores. If tie still remains,
  • Average of creativity appeal scores. If tie still remains,
  • Average of presentation appeal scores. If tie still remains,
  • Majority of the judges' votes by raising hands for one of the teams with the same number of points. (+0.5 points for the team with the most votes).
